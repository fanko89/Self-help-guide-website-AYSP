Duct sizing is the bridge between equipment and comfort.
What duct sizing tries to achieve
  • Enough airflow (CFM) to each room
  • Quiet operation (low noise at registers)
  • Reasonable static pressure so the blower is not overworked
  • Even temperatures (reduced hot/cold rooms)
Common problems this fixes
  • Bedrooms too hot in summer or too cold in winter
  • Noisy returns or whistling supplies
  • Dusty rooms due to leaks and bypass
  • Short cycling from restricted airflow
Static pressure - the number that proves airflow

Static pressure is measured with a manometer and tells you whether the duct system is allowing the right airflow. It is one of the most important post-install verification measurements.
