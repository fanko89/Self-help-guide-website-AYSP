Why brand names matter less than installation

Two homes can buy the same brand and get very different results. The difference is sizing, airflow, duct design, refrigerant charge, and verification. A premium system installed poorly will still short cycle, run loud, and waste energy.

How to read reviews without getting fooled

Reviews can help, but process and proof matter more than star count.

Video: How to spot shortcut companies

Purpose: show how to spot vague sales language vs measurable process.

What actually matters inside reviews
  • Comfort results described (hot/cold rooms improved, quieter system)
  • Clear communication and clean jobsite
  • Follow-up support or warranty help
  • Mentions of diagnosing the real cause, not just swapping equipment
Open review signals
Red flags you should not ignore
  • Only price or brand talk with no mention of outcomes
  • Sales pressure or rushed decisions
  • Overly vague reviews that all sound the same
  • Repeated complaints about missed appointments or poor follow-up
Open red flags
Google vs Yelp (what most people miss)
  • Google reviews are easy to leave and are not tied to a verified job.
  • Yelp reviews are more often tied to a real service interaction and reviewer history.
  • Star count alone does not prove correct design, sizing, or verification.
  • Use reviews as a starting point, then verify the process.
The best reviews mention measurements, not just friendliness.
